# summary

**Threads (social network)**  
Threads is a text-based social networking platform developed by Meta Platforms, Inc., the parent company of Instagram and Facebook. Launched on July 5, 2023, the app allows users to share text posts, images, and videos, offering features such as replying, reposting, and liking content. Closely integrated with Instagram, Threads requires users to have an Instagram account and use the same handle, streamlining onboarding and leveraging Instagram's extensive user base. Designed to rival X (formerly Twitter), Threads has positioned itself as a platform for conversational engagement and text-based interactions within a familiar social media ecosystem.[1]  
Upon its launch, Threads achieved historic success, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by amassing over 100 million users within five days. This rapid adoption eclipsed the record previously set by ChatGPT, highlighting strong initial demand for an alternative to established social media platforms. However, sustaining user engagement proved challenging; by the end of July 2023, Threads experienced a steep decline in daily active users, dropping by more than 80% to approximately 8 million. The platform's struggle to retain users has been attributed to limited features and its heavy reliance on Instagram, raising questions about its long-term viability in the competitive social networking market.[1]  
Threads' launch also sparked controversy over its data collection practices. Its release in the European Union was delayed due to regulatory concerns under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Critics have expressed apprehension about the app's compliance with privacy regulations and the extent of its integration with Instagram. Despite these challenges, Meta has signaled its commitment to expanding the app’s functionality and addressing user feedback, including plans to enhance its web experience and pursue regulatory clarity for a future EU launch.[1]  
As a product of Meta’s broader strategy to diversify its social networking offerings, Threads has contributed to ongoing discussions about user preferences, platform innovation, and the shifting dynamics of the social media landscape. Launched amid major industry changes, including Elon Musk’s acquisition of Twitter, Threads reflects Meta's efforts to capture a share of the text-based social networking market while navigating the challenges of user retention and regulatory compliance.[1]

# History

## Predecessor

The origins of Threads can be traced back to October 2019, when Meta Platforms (then Facebook, Inc.) introduced a separate app called Threads. This app was designed to complement Instagram's "Close Friends" feature, offering users the ability to communicate privately through messaging, images, and video chats. The platform integrated Instagram's photo editing tools, focusing on creating a more intimate space for communication. However, due to low adoption rates—only around 220,000 global downloads, which accounted for less than 0.1% of Instagram's monthly active users—and the migration of most of its features to Instagram, the original Threads app was discontinued in December 2021[1].

## Development and Announcement

Following Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter in October 2022, Meta began exploring ways to introduce text-based functionalities to its existing platforms. This led to the release of Instagram Notes in December 2022, a feature allowing users to post short text updates. Building on this concept, Meta initiated the development of a standalone text-based social media app. Dubbed "Project 92" internally, the development of what would become Threads began in January 2023. The platform was officially launched on July 5, 2023, making it available in 100 countries at launch. However, its release in the European Union was delayed as the company awaited further regulatory clarity from the European Commission regarding its data collection policies[1].
Threads experienced unprecedented initial success, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history. Within five days of its release, the app had amassed over 100 million users, surpassing the record previously set by ChatGPT. Despite this explosive growth, Threads struggled to maintain user engagement, with its daily active user base declining by more than 80% to approximately 8 million by the end of July 2023[1].

# Features

Threads is an online social networking platform that allows users to post and share various types of content, including text, images, and videos[1]. The app supports interactive features such as replying to posts, reposting, and liking, creating an engaging social experience similar to X (formerly known as Twitter)[1]. Threads is closely integrated with Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and use the same handle for both platforms[1].
The application is available for iOS and Android devices, and its web version offers limited functionality, necessitating the initial installation of the mobile app for full access[1]. Threads' design and functionality make it accessible for users who are already familiar with Instagram, while focusing on text-based interactions to differentiate it from its parent platform[1].

# Integration with Other Platforms

Threads operates as a separate app but is closely integrated with Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and use the same Instagram handle for Threads[1]. This integration allows seamless sharing and interaction between the two platforms, enhancing user experience by leveraging Instagram’s existing user base and infrastructure. Users can easily follow their Instagram connections on Threads, fostering an interconnected social networking ecosystem[1].
Despite its reliance on Instagram for account creation and user identity, Threads offers functionality similar to X (formerly known as Twitter), enabling users to post and engage with text-based content, images, and videos independently of Instagram[1]. This reflects Meta's strategic positioning of Threads as a complementary service to its flagship platforms, primarily Instagram, rather than a standalone competitor to other social networking sites[1].

# User Base

Threads experienced an unprecedented surge in user adoption following its launch on July 5, 2023, quickly becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history. The platform garnered over 100 million users within its first five days, surpassing the previous record set by ChatGPT[1]. This meteoric growth was attributed to its seamless integration with Instagram, as users were required to have an Instagram account and use Threads under the same handle, facilitating swift onboarding[1].
However, the initial success of Threads proved difficult to sustain. By the end of July 2023, the platform's daily active user base had dropped by more than 80%, declining to approximately 8 million users. This sharp reduction highlighted challenges in retaining engagement despite the app’s early popularity[1].

# Controversies and Criticism

Threads faced significant scrutiny over its data collection policies, particularly in the context of its delayed launch in the European Union. Meta Platforms, the operator of Threads, opted to withhold the app's release in EU member states due to the lack of regulatory clarity from the European Commission regarding its data practices. Concerns were raised about the potential scope of user data collection and its compliance with the EU's stringent privacy regulations, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) [1].
Critics also highlighted the app's heavy reliance on Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and use the same handle for Threads. While this integration aimed to streamline user onboarding, some users expressed concerns about the lack of flexibility and autonomy in creating separate identities across platforms [1].

# Impact and Influence

Threads achieved a remarkable milestone in the technology industry by becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history. The platform gained over 100 million users within its first five days of launch, surpassing the previous record held by ChatGPT[1]. Its early growth underscored the strong demand for an alternative to existing social networking platforms, particularly as Threads positioned itself as a competitor to X (formerly known as Twitter). The platform's rapid adoption highlighted Meta's ability to leverage its Instagram ecosystem[1].
Despite its initial success, Threads faced significant challenges in sustaining user engagement. By the end of July 2023, the app's daily active user base had declined by over 80%, dropping to just 8 million[1]. This decline raised questions about Threads' long-term viability and the ability of new social networking services to maintain user interest in a competitive and evolving digital landscape.
Threads' integration with Instagram and its text-based focus has contributed to discussions about the convergence of social media functionalities. Its launch came at a time when the social networking industry was experiencing shifts due to major events, including Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter in October 2022[1].

# Reception

Threads experienced a meteoric rise in its initial launch phase, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by acquiring over 100 million users within its first five days of availability[1]. However, the platform's early success was short-lived, as its user base significantly declined by the end of July 2023. Daily active users dropped by over 80%, from its initial peak to approximately 8 million[1].
Critics and analysts attributed this sharp decline in user engagement to limited features and functionality compared to its primary competitor, X (formerly Twitter), as well as the necessity of an Instagram account to use the service[1]. Moreover, concerns were raised about its data collection policies, delaying its launch in the European Union pending regulatory clarity from the European Commission[1].

# Future Developments

Meta Platforms has signaled its commitment to expanding Threads by addressing user feedback and enhancing platform functionality. In particular, the company has indicated plans to introduce a more robust web experience, moving beyond the current limitations that require initial mobile app installation and provide restricted access to features[1]. Additionally, Meta is exploring ways to retain and grow its user base following a significant drop in daily active users, which fell by more than 80% to 8 million by the end of July 2023[1].
The platform’s delayed launch in the European Union also highlights a key area of development. Meta has been engaging with the European Commission to ensure compliance with data collection policies in line with EU regulations. Once clarity is achieved, Threads is expected to expand its availability to the EU market, potentially driving growth in user adoption across Europe[1].
