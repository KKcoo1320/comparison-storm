# summary

OceanGate Inc. is an American company based in Everett, Washington, specializing in crewed submersibles for tourism, research, and exploration since its founding in 2009 by Stockton Rush and Guillermo Söhnlein. The company gained notoriety for its expeditions to the Titanic wreck site aboard its submersible Titan, which tragically imploded during a voyage in June 2023, resulting in the deaths of all five occupants. # Overview

OceanGate Inc. is an American privately owned company based in Everett, Washington, that specializes in providing crewed submersibles for tourism, industry, research, and exploration. Founded in 2009 by Stockton Rush and Guillermo Söhnlein, the company initially acquired a submersible vessel named Antipodes and later developed its own models, including Cyclops 1 and Titan. In 2021, OceanGate began offering paid expeditions for tourists aboard the Titan to visit the wreck of the Titanic, with ticket prices reaching US$250,000 per person by 2022 [1]. Tragically, on June 18, 2023, the Titan imploded during a voyage to the Titanic wreck site, resulting in the deaths of all five individuals on board, including Stockton Rush. Following this incident, an extensive international search and rescue operation was conducted, and the wreckage was located on June 22, approximately 500 meters (1,600 ft) from the Titanic wreck site. Subsequently, on July 6, 2023, OceanGate announced the suspension of all its operations [1].
