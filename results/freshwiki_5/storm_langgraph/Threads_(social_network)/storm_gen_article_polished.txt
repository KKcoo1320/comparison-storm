# summary

Threads is a social media platform launched by Meta Platforms on July 5, 2023, designed for text-based communication and closely integrated with Instagram. It allows users to share text, images, and videos while engaging in real-time conversations through replies, reposts, and likes. Despite achieving rapid initial growth, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history with over 100 million users in its first five days, Threads has faced significant challenges in maintaining user engagement, with daily active users dropping by more than 80% shortly after launch.

# 1. Overview

## 1.1 Definition and Purpose

Threads is an online social media and social networking service operated by Meta Platforms, designed to allow users to post and share text, images, and videos. It facilitates interaction through replies, reposts, and likes, creating a platform for real-time conversation and engagement similar to that of X (formerly known as Twitter) [1]. The app is closely linked to Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and operate Threads under the same handle. Launched on July 5, 2023, Threads aims to provide a space for users to connect and communicate in a text-based format, expanding the functionalities of Instagram beyond visual content [1].

## 1.2 Key Differentiators from Other Platforms

One of the key differentiators of Threads is its integration with Instagram, leveraging the existing user base and familiarity of Instagram's interface [1]. Additionally, Threads achieved remarkable initial growth, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by amassing over 100 million users within its first five days of launch, surpassing the previous record held by ChatGPT [1]. However, despite this rapid growth, the platform experienced a significant decline in user engagement, with daily active users dropping by more than 80% shortly after its launch [1].

# 2. Development

## 2.1 Launch Timeline

Threads was officially launched on July 5, 2023. The development of the app, internally referred to as "Project 92," began in January 2023, following the exploration of text-based features for Instagram after Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter in October 2022. The app quickly gained traction, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by amassing over 100 million users within its first five days. However, this rapid growth was not sustained, with the user base declining by more than 80% to approximately 8 million daily active users by the end of July 2023 [1].

## 2.2 Integration with Instagram

Threads is closely integrated with Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and use the same handle on both platforms. This integration allows for seamless sharing of content and interactions between the two services, enhancing user engagement. The functionality of Threads is designed to mirror that of X, enabling users to post and share text, images, and videos, as well as interact through replies, reposts, and likes [1].

## 2.3 Regulatory Considerations

While Threads was made available in 100 countries at launch, its rollout in the European Union has been delayed due to ongoing regulatory considerations. Meta Platforms is awaiting clarity from the European Commission regarding the app's data collection policies, which are critical for compliance with EU regulations [1].

# 3. Features

## 3.1 Text-Based Focus

Threads is primarily designed for text-based communication, allowing users to post and share text, images, and videos. This focus aligns the platform closely with its predecessor, Twitter, and is part of Meta's strategy to capture users seeking a microblogging experience [1].

## 3.2 User Interaction Options

The platform provides various user interaction options, including replies, reposts, and likes, enabling users to engage with content shared by others. This interactive functionality encourages community engagement and facilitates conversations around shared interests [1].

## 3.3 Limited Web Functionality

While Threads is available on iOS and Android devices, its web version offers limited functionality. Users must first install the mobile app to access the full range of features, which may restrict the user experience for those preferring desktop access [1].

## 3.4 Global Availability

Upon its launch, Threads became available in 100 countries. However, its rollout in the European Union has been delayed as Meta seeks regulatory clarity regarding data collection policies [1].

# 4. User Experience

## 4.1 User Demographics

Threads requires users to have an Instagram account, significantly influencing its user demographics. The app attracted a diverse user base, primarily consisting of younger individuals who are already active on Instagram, fostering a seamless transition for existing users to engage in text-based discussions [1].

## 4.2 User Engagement Metrics

Upon its release, Threads experienced unprecedented growth, amassing over 100 million users within its first five days [1]. However, by the end of July 2023, the platform saw a dramatic decline, with daily active users dropping by more than 80% to approximately 8 million [1]. This fluctuation raises questions about the app's long-term viability and user retention strategies.

## 4.3 Strategies for User Retention

To counteract the decline in active users, Threads may need to implement strategies aimed at enhancing user retention. These could include introducing new features that encourage daily interaction, such as enhanced content discovery tools, personalized feeds, and community-building functionalities. Additionally, leveraging Instagram's existing user base to promote cross-platform engagement could help maintain user interest [1].

# 5. Reception

## 5.1 Initial Growth and User Adoption

Threads experienced unprecedented initial growth, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history. Within just five days, it amassed over 100 million users, largely attributed to its integration with Instagram [1].

## 5.2 Decline in Daily Active Users

Despite its explosive start, Threads faced a significant decline in user engagement shortly after its launch. By the end of July 2023, the app's daily active users plummeted by more than 80%, raising concerns about the platform's long-term viability [1].

## 5.3 Comparison with Other Social Networks

In comparison to other social networks, Threads' initial growth was remarkable, yet its subsequent drop in daily active users highlighted challenges similar to those faced by other platforms. The rapid rise and fall of Threads serve as a case study in the volatile nature of social media user retention [1].
