# 1. Overview

## 1.1 Definition and Purpose

Threads is an online social media and social networking service operated by Meta Platforms, designed to allow users to post and share text, images, and videos. It facilitates interaction through replies, reposts, and likes, creating a platform for real-time conversation and engagement similar to that of X (formerly known as Twitter) [1]. The app is closely linked to Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and operate Threads under the same handle. Launched on July 5, 2023, Threads aims to provide a space for users to connect and communicate in a text-based format, expanding the functionalities of Instagram beyond visual content [1].

## 1.2 Key Differentiators from Other Platforms

One of the key differentiators of Threads from other social media platforms is its integration with Instagram, leveraging the existing user base and familiarity of Instagram's interface [1]. Additionally, Threads achieved remarkable initial growth, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by amassing over 100 million users within its first five days of launch, surpassing the previous record held by ChatGPT [1]. However, despite this rapid growth, the platform experienced a significant decline in user engagement, with daily active users dropping by more than 80% shortly after its launch [1].

# 2. Development

## 2.1 Launch Timeline

Threads, a social media platform developed by Meta Platforms, was officially launched on July 5, 2023. The development of the app, internally referred to as "Project 92," began in January 2023, following the exploration of text-based features for Instagram after Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter in October 2022. The app quickly gained traction, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history by amassing over 100 million users within its first five days, a record previously held by ChatGPT. However, this rapid growth was not sustained, with the user base declining by more than 80% to approximately 8 million daily active users by the end of July 2023 [1].

## 2.2 Integration with Instagram

Threads is closely integrated with Instagram, requiring users to have an Instagram account and use the same handle on both platforms. This integration allows for seamless sharing of content and interactions between the two services, enhancing user engagement. The functionality of Threads is designed to mirror that of X (formerly known as Twitter), enabling users to post and share text, images, and videos, as well as interact through replies, reposts, and likes [1].

## 2.3 Regulatory Considerations

While Threads was made available in 100 countries at launch, its rollout in the European Union has been delayed due to ongoing regulatory considerations. Meta Platforms is awaiting clarity from the European Commission regarding the app's data collection policies, which are critical for compliance with EU regulations. This cautious approach reflects the company's strategy to navigate the complex regulatory landscape surrounding data privacy and user rights in the region [1].

# 5. Reception

## 5.1 Initial Growth and User Adoption

Threads, launched by Meta Platforms on July 5, 2023, experienced unprecedented initial growth, becoming the fastest-growing consumer software application in history. Within just five days, it amassed over 100 million users, surpassing the previous record held by ChatGPT [1]. This rapid adoption was largely attributed to its integration with Instagram, requiring users to have an existing Instagram account to join Threads, which facilitated a quick influx of users eager to explore the new platform.

## 5.2 Decline in Daily Active Users

Despite its explosive start, Threads faced a significant decline in user engagement shortly after its launch. By the end of July 2023, the app's daily active users plummeted by more than 80%, dropping to approximately 8 million [1]. This decline raised concerns about the platform's long-term viability and its ability to retain users in a competitive social media landscape.

## 5.3 Comparison with Other Social Networks

In comparison to other social networks, Threads' initial growth was remarkable, yet its subsequent drop in daily active users highlighted challenges similar to those faced by other platforms. For instance, while Threads aimed to position itself as a text-based alternative to Twitter (now X), it struggled to maintain user engagement in the face of established competitors. The rapid rise and fall of Threads serve as a case study in the volatile nature of social media user retention and the complexities of sustaining user interest in a crowded market [1].
