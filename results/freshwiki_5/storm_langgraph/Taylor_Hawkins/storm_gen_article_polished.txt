# summary

Oliver Taylor Hawkins (1972–2022) was an influential American drummer best known for his work with the rock band Foo Fighters, where he contributed to eight studio albums and became a key figure in the band's success. Born in Fort Worth, Texas, and raised in California, Hawkins began his musical career in the mid-1990s, gaining recognition as a drummer for Alanis Morissette before joining the Foo Fighters in 1997. His dynamic drumming style, energetic performances, and versatility as a musician earned him accolades, including being named "Best Rock Drummer" by *Rhythm* magazine in 2005 and an induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2021.

# Early Life

Oliver Taylor Hawkins was born in Fort Worth, Texas, on February 17, 1972. His family moved to Laguna Beach, California, in 1976, where Hawkins grew up [1]. He was the youngest of three siblings, with an older brother and sister named Jason and Heather [1]. Hawkins graduated from Laguna Beach High School in 1990, where he formed friendships with notable figures, including Jon Davison, the current lead vocalist of the progressive rock band Yes [1]. His early exposure to music and the vibrant California culture played a significant role in shaping his future career as a drummer.

# Musical Career

## Early Career

Oliver Taylor Hawkins began his musical journey in the Orange County-based band Sylvia before joining Sass Jordan as her drummer. His significant break came when he was recruited by Alanis Morissette, serving as her drummer from June 1995 until March 1997 during the tours for her acclaimed album *Jagged Little Pill*. Hawkins contributed to several of Morissette's music videos, including "You Oughta Know" and "You Learn," and appeared in her live VHS/DVD *Jagged Little Pill, Live* (1997) [1]. His exceptional drumming skills earned him recognition, as he was voted "Best Rock Drummer" in 2005 by the British drumming magazine *Rhythm* [1].

## Joining Foo Fighters

In 1997, Taylor Hawkins officially joined the Foo Fighters as their drummer, a position he held until his untimely death in 2022. His entry into the band came after a successful stint as the drummer for Alanis Morissette, during which he gained significant recognition for his energetic performances and technical skills [1]. Hawkins quickly became an integral part of the Foo Fighters' sound and identity, contributing to their rise in popularity throughout the late 1990s and 2000s.

## Contributions to eight studio albums (1999-2021)

Hawkins contributed to eight studio albums from 1999 to 2021, showcasing his dynamic drumming style and vocal abilities. His first album with the band, "There Is Nothing Left to Lose" (1999), marked the beginning of a successful collaboration that continued with subsequent releases, including "One by One" (2002), "In Your Honor" (2005), "Echoes, Silence, Patience & Grace" (2007), "Wasting Light" (2011), "Sonic Highways" (2014), "Concrete and Gold" (2017), and "Medicine at Midnight" (2021) [1][2]. In addition to his work with Foo Fighters, Hawkins was involved in various side projects and collaborations that further showcased his musical versatility.

## Side Projects and Collaborations

### Formation of Taylor Hawkins and the Coattail Riders (2004)

In 2004, Taylor Hawkins established his side project, Taylor Hawkins and the Coattail Riders. This venture allowed Hawkins to explore his musical creativity beyond the confines of his primary band. The project was characterized by a blend of rock and pop influences, showcasing Hawkins' talents not only as a drummer but also as a vocalist and songwriter. The self-titled debut album was released in 2006, followed by two more studio albums: *Red Light Fever* in 2010 and *Get the Money* in 2019 [1][2].

### Co-founding the supergroup NHC (2020)

In 2020, Taylor Hawkins co-founded the supergroup NHC alongside Jane's Addiction members Dave Navarro and Chris Chaney. In this collaboration, Hawkins took on dual roles as both the drummer and lead vocalist, showcasing his versatility as a musician beyond his well-known work with the Foo Fighters [1][2].

### Notable collaborations

Hawkins was involved in numerous notable collaborations throughout his career, showcasing his versatility as a drummer and musician. He toured with prominent artists such as Alanis Morissette, where he played a significant role in her live performances [1]. He also participated in various supergroups, including NHC, formed in 2020 with members of Jane's Addiction, where he took on both lead vocal and drumming responsibilities [1]. In 2007, Hawkins was part of the SOS Allstars, a collective that included Roger Taylor of Queen and Chad Smith of the Red Hot Chili Peppers, performing at the Live Earth concert [1]. His drumming contributions extended to the album "Good Apollo, I'm Burning Star IV, Volume Two: No World for Tomorrow" by Coheed and Cambria, where he recorded drum tracks due to the absence of their regular drummer, Chris Pennie [1].
